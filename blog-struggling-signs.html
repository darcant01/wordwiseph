Why Is My Child Struggling with English? 7 Signs and Simple Solutions

📖 7 min read · July 2026 · WordWise PH Team
← Back to Blog

If you suspect your child is struggling with English, trust that instinct — parents usually sense it before report cards confirm it. The good news: caught early, every one of these problems is fixable at home. Here are the 7 most common warning signs and exactly what to do about each.

Sign 1: Avoiding English Entirely

Your child switches to Tagalog the moment English gets hard, or goes quiet when asked to speak English.

Solution: Lower the stakes. Start with low-pressure activities — English cartoons, songs, or games where mistakes do not feel embarrassing. Confidence comes before competence.

Sign 2: Reading Aloud is Painful

Word-by-word reading, frequent stumbles, or guessing words based on the first letter.

Solution: Drop to easier books — reading should feel 90% smooth. Re-read favorites (repetition builds fluency). Ten minutes daily beats an hour weekly.

Sign 3: Grades Dropping in Other Subjects Too

Science and Math are taught in English — if English is weak, everything suffers. This is often mistaken for struggling in Math when it is actually a language issue.

Solution: Check if word problems are the issue. If your child can compute 25 + 17 but fails "Juan has 25 mangoes..." — it is English comprehension, not math.

Sign 4: Memorizing Instead of Understanding

Your child can recite grammar rules but cannot use them in a sentence. They pass quizzes by memorizing but freeze in real conversation.

Solution: Shift from rules to usage. Games and conversation force real application. "Use it in a sentence" beats "recite the rule" every time.

Sign 5: Writing Much Worse Than Speaking

They chat comfortably but their writing is full of errors, or they write painfully short answers to avoid mistakes.

Solution: Separate ideas from correctness. First draft = just write freely. Second pass = fix together. Correcting while writing kills both skills.

Sign 6: Frustration or Tears at Homework Time

English homework triggers meltdowns, procrastination, or "I am not smart" statements.

Solution: This is emotional, not academic. Praise effort loudly, treat errors as normal ("Even I make that mistake!"), and switch to game-based practice where failing costs nothing.

Sign 7: Teacher Mentions Participation Issues

The teacher says your child never raises their hand or avoids speaking in English class — even when they know the answer.

Solution: Practice speaking at home where it is safe. Family "English hour" with zero corrections builds the confidence to speak up in class.

💡 Remember: Struggling with English is incredibly common and highly fixable. The kids who improve fastest are not the smartest — they are the ones who practice a little bit every day without fear of mistakes.
