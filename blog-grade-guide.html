Grade 1–6 English Grammar Topics: What Your Child Should Know Per Grade

📖 6 min read · July 2026 · WordWise PH Team
← Back to Blog

Wondering if your child is on track? This grade-by-grade checklist shows exactly which grammar skills students typically master at each level. Use it to spot gaps early — catching a weak area one grade late is easy to fix; catching it three grades late is much harder.

Grade 1: Building Blocks

  • Naming words (nouns) — people, places, things
  • Action words (verbs) — run, jump, eat
  • Simple sentences: "The dog runs."
  • Capital letters at the start, period at the end
  • Singular vs plural with -s (cat → cats)
💡 At this age, focus on speaking and recognizing — writing rules come later.

Grade 2: Expanding Sentences

  • Describing words (adjectives) — big, red, happy
  • Pronouns: he, she, it, they (extra practice needed for Filipino kids!)
  • Present tense vs past tense basics
  • Question words: who, what, where, when, why
  • Using "a" and "an" correctly

Grade 3: Grammar Rules Kick In

  • Subject-verb agreement (she walks, they walk)
  • Irregular past tense (go → went, eat → ate)
  • Prepositions: in, on, under, beside
  • Compound sentences with "and," "but," "or"
  • Contractions: don't, can't, isn't

Grade 4: Getting Precise

  • All verb tenses: past, present, future
  • Adverbs: quickly, carefully, always
  • Comparatives and superlatives (big, bigger, biggest)
  • Possessives: the dog's bone, the girls' bags
  • Paragraph structure basics

Grade 5: Complex Structures

  • Perfect tenses (has eaten, had gone)
  • Direct and indirect speech
  • Conjunctions: because, although, however
  • Active vs passive voice introduction
  • Common idioms and expressions

Grade 6: Mastery and Style

  • All 8 parts of speech confidently
  • Complex and compound-complex sentences
  • Subject-verb agreement in tricky cases
  • Formal vs informal language
  • Editing their own writing for grammar
💡 Falling behind is normal and fixable. If your Grade 5 child struggles with Grade 3 topics, just start there. Grammar builds like stairs — you cannot skip steps, but you can climb fast with daily practice.
